# Content Rules - GuiselleArmstrong.com

## Content Sourcing Rules

### PRIMARY SOURCE OF TRUTH
All professional data **MUST** come from the `profile-data/` directory. Never fabricate or invent:
- Job titles, dates, or responsibilities
- Certifications or certifications
- Education details
- Skills or achievements
- Contact information

### Required Fields for Employment Entries
When creating or updating employment content, include:
- Employer name (exact spelling from source data)
- Job title (exact title from source)
- Start and end dates (format: "Mar 2023", "June 2023 - October 2025")
- Location (city, state/country)
- Bullet points with measurable achievements (include metrics where available)

### Achievement Metrics
Always preserve and highlight numerical achievements:
- "Reduced compliance violations by 50%"
- "Managed vulnerability lifecycle for 500+ enterprise systems"
- "95% remediation rate within defined SLAs"
- "Improved change success rates from 85% to 98%"

### Content Tone and Style
- **Professional but approachable** - This is a job-seeking portfolio
- **Action-oriented** - Use strong verbs (Led, Managed, Designed, Reduced, Improved)
- **Quantified** - Include metrics and numbers wherever possible
- **Concise** - Avoid fluff; every line should demonstrate value

### Language Rules
- spellcheck all content
- Use American English spelling
- Keep sentences under 30 words where possible
- Use bullet points for lists of achievements

### Sections to Prioritize
1. **Homepage** - Professional summary with key differentiators
2. **Experience** - Detailed employment history with metrics
3. **Skills** - Organized by category (Core Competencies, Technical, Frameworks, Tools)
4. **Certifications** - All 8 certifications listed clearly
5. **Education** - Degrees with expected/completed status

### Content to Avoid
- Generic placeholder text from the Hugo theme
- Fictional achievements or inflated claims
- Overly technical jargon without context
- Personal information beyond professional contact details
